[[section-technical-risks]]
== Risks and Technical Debts

=== Technical risks

[Attributes]
|===
|Risk |Description

|Flutter abandoned
|Google has somewhat of a history of abandoning it’s projects. 
Evaluating possible migration scenarios to e.g. web-native may be more sustainable.

|Cloud - Cost Management
|After deployment to one cloud provider, swapping the provider can be difficult, if the provider raises the prices. 
Using Infrastructure as Code and keeping things generic eases a possible migration.

|High Dependecy on Kafka MQ
|The system heavily relies on Kafka MQ for any internal communication. If the project is abandoned or a breaking bug is implemented it could present a detrimental threat to our operation. Forcing us to refactor big sections of out code base.

|Pixlr dependency
|The platform relies on Pixlr to be able to edit photos, if the service is unavailable or discontinued it can impact a crucial functionality of our system. 

|===

=== Business or domain risks

[Attributes]
|===
|Risk |Description

|Low Userbase
|The product is reliant on user interactions and uploads. In case of problems of attracting a big userbase the popularity can dwindle fast, resulting in costs could be higher than the generated income.

|Subscription functions not well received
|If the pay-to-use functionality does not offer the benefits our users are willing to pay for, our financing plan could fail.

|===
