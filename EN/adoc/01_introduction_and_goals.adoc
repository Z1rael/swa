=== Quality Goals

ifdef::arc42help[]
[role="arc42help"]
****
.Contents
The top three (max five) quality goals for the architecture whose fulfillment is of highest importance to the major stakeholders. 
We really mean quality goals for the architecture. Don't confuse them with project goals.
They are not necessarily identical.

Consider this overview of potential topics (based upon the ISO 25010 standard):

image::01_2_iso-25010-topics-EN-2023.drawio.png["Categories of Quality Requirements"]

.Motivation
You should know the quality goals of your most important stakeholders, since they will influence fundamental architectural decisions. 
Make sure to be very concrete about these qualities, avoid buzzwords.
If you as an architect do not know how the quality of your work will be judged...

.Form
A table with quality goals and concrete scenarios, ordered by priorities
****
endif::arc42help[]

[options="header",cols="1,1,2,4"]
|===
|Priority|Key|Quality|Motivation
| 1 | | Usability | High usability is a must, if we want to be competitive against the existing platform giants.
| 2 | | Scalability | Because of our size it is important, that we can scale to serve a massive international audience.
| 3 | | Auditability | Because of our international operation, auditability is important to be able to respect the various laws.
| 4 | | Reliability | Reliability is important, because nothing is more annoying to an end user than a page that does not work.
| 5 | | Portability | To reach the largest audience it is important, that the application can run on most devices.
|===

=== Stakeholders

ifdef::arc42help[]
[role="arc42help"]
****
.Contents
Explicit overview of stakeholders of the system, i.e. all person, roles or organizations that

* should know the architecture
* have to be convinced of the architecture
* have to work with the architecture or with code
* need the documentation of the architecture for their work
* have to come up with decisions about the system or its development

.Motivation
You should know all parties involved in development of the system or affected by the system.
Otherwise, you may get nasty surprises later in the development process.
These stakeholders determine the extent and the level of detail of your work and its results.

.Form
Table with role names, person names, and their expectations with respect to the architecture and its documentation.
****
endif::arc42help[]

[options="header",cols="1,2,2"]
|===
|Role/Name|Contact|Expectations

| User
| End users (students / public users)
| Wants to share, edit and interact with images, wants the application to be fast and usable on different platforms

| Admin
| System administrators / moderation team 
| Wants to have the necessary tools to moderate the platform, curate a collection of highlighted images and remove problematic content

| Developer
| development team 
| Wants to deliver modular features fast and safely, maintain a clean and extensible architecture

| Influencer
| Content creators / social media users
| Wants to build an audience, attract other users, grow a community and potentially monetize content

| Legal Team
| University compliance / legal advisory (GDPR / copyright compliance)
| Wants the application to adhere to international and local legal requirements to avoid lawsuits, especially regarding personal data and licensing (copyright)
|===
